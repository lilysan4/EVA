How did “EVA” begin?

Not everything starts as a fully formed idea. Some things begin as a trace, a conversation, a quiet moment… and slowly take shape over time.

We are a Christian Brazilian family, originally from Porto Alegre, Rio Grande do Sul.

For as long as we can remember, we used to talk about financial inequality in the world — how some people have so much, while others have almost nothing. People who struggle to afford basic needs, like feeding their families or accessing medical treatment, often live just steps away from those who have more than enough. And yet… why do these worlds coexist without truly seeing each other’s needs? That question stayed with us.

In 2024, our lives changed completely when we migrated to another country to serve as Missionaries. We left everything behind — family, friends, our home, and everything we had built over the years — and started over from zero in Tampa, Florida. With the initial help of a relative already living here, we were given the chance to experience a completely new world. A new social life. New friends. New family connections from different nationalities — people we had no blood relation to, yet who welcomed us as if we did. Helping hands that made sure we could stand steady again.

And it was inside this new world, surrounded by new experiences and perspectives, that the question returned — louder than before. Because the best ideas are born from questions: “What if there was a way to connect those who need help with those who are willing to help?” That question stayed with me for a long time before I understood what I needed to do with it.

Then, on June 28th, 2026, at the dinner table, We — The Santos Family — decided we would create the answer to our own question.

Why “EVA”?

Eva is the name of my late and deeply loved grandmother.

She was a Christian woman — a true prayer warrior — someone who loved with a kind of depth that is hard to describe in words. She had a huge heart and prayed every night for her children, grandchildren, great-grandchildren, and for everyone who was part of her family and circle of friends.

For ten years, we lived on the same property. My childhood was filled with moments spent running to her house, as if it were an extension of my own. In many ways, it felt like I lived there just as much as I lived in my own home.

Her passing left a mark on my family and on me — a wound that will never fully disappear.

So in the very moment I realized that the biggest project of my life needed a name, I knew it had to be hers.

EVA is not just a name. It is memory. It is love. It is legacy.
